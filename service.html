Health and safety

Statement of Intent

The health and safety of our children, staff and visitors is of utmost importance.

Aim

The policy of child's play:- Strive to achieve an environment that constantly offers the highest possible level of health and safety in all activities and operations and comply with health and safety laws.

First Aid

Supplies are readily available in all classrooms and staff are trained and certified in First Aid and CPR.

Emergencies

Procedures for handling emergencies are posted in classrooms as well as schedules of practice drills to prepare children and staff for an emergency

Safety Checklist

Infants and young children are at risk of injury everyday in classrooms, staff complete a health and safety checklist twice annually to ensure a safe learning environment.

Illnesses

Since illnesses pose risk to health of others, the program's illness policy provide guideline for child attendance at the centre. Parents are also notified if a child in care contracted a communicable disease.

Accident Book

Is available on the premises and all accidents has to be completed in the accident book.

Outdoor Area Safety Measures

Hygiene

We have a daily cleansing routine for the pre-school which includes play rooms, kitchen, rest area, toilets and nappy changing areas. The toilet area has a high standard of hygiene including hand washing and drying facilities and the disposal of nappies.

Activities

All equipment is regularly checked for cleanliness, safety and dangerous items are repaired or discarded. All materials- including paint and glue which are non-toxic. All toys are safe and age appropriate.

Food And Drink

Staff who handle food receive appropriate training and comply with food safety and hygiene. Waste is disposed of properly and out of reach of children.

Our School Policy

  • The name(s) of the people who drops off and picks up the child(ren).
  • That children are supervised at all times.
  • Daily health checks.
  • Procedures to follow if abuse is or neglect is suspected.

Guidelines for giving medicine to children.

  • All medicines must be labelled and in the original container with the name of doctor or clinic.
  • The container must be labelled with the child’s name, surname, expiring date, and the dosage to be given. We need to be careful because more than one child may be taking the same medicine.
  • How many times in a day he/she should take it.
  • We do not use any medicine after the expiry date.

Childhood emergencies.

  • We need to keep anything that can harm children out of their reach and in a locked cupboard.
  • This include all cleaning products and medicine.
  • We need to check the play area indoors and outdoors and the equipment and toys for broken nails, pieces of glass and sharp objects.
  • We need to cover all wall plugs.
  • We explain to children why they should not eat or drink while running or playing and why they should not put small objects into their mouth.

Knowing how to handle emergencies.

  • When accidents occur we need to know what to do, teachers need to have basic skills in FIRST AID so that they can deal immediately. All staff should have an up-to-date certificate in FIRST AID.
  • Know who to call for help: the nearest ambulance, doctor or clinic.
  • Know evacuation procedures in case of fire.

Smoke-free

  • Smoking will NOT be permitted (this includes e-cigarettes) in any part of the school premises..
  • This school is a NO smoking- school and this should be made clear within all its appointments procedures, staff handbooks and the school prospectus.

Decleration of childrens rights

All children have the right to a name, enough food to eat, and a decent place to live.
Children with barriers to learning, need their homes adapted for special needs learning and children with special diets, need to be catered for.
All children should be looked after when they are sick, and they the right to grow up with love, affection and security.
Children with special needs has the right to health care and to be loved.
Handicapped children have a right to special treatment and education.
We make our centre wheelchair friendly for leanrers with special needs to have full access to education.
All children have a right to free education and should be protected from neglect and exploitation.
Children with barriers to learning must be helped to learn so that they can have equal opportunities for education and inclusion into society.
All children should not be made to work before a certain age.
All children including those with barriers to learning have a right to basic education.
All children should not have to fear arrest and detention.
Children with special needs should not be left unattended in a backroom of their house or in places where there are no possibilities for growth and development.
All children should understand that their energy and talents should be devoted to the service of their brothers and sisters.
We should cheer for the child with special needs… and they will cheer for you!
